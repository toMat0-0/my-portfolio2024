About This Porject

This project started during the second semester of my master’s program in machine learning. One day, I was reading a paper about using statistical models to generate the sounds of ancient languages. It wasn’t directly related to anything I was working on, but it sparked something—and then my golden retriever roommate, Marley, gave me the perfect idea. Marley doesn’t talk (of course), but he communicates all the time: barking excitedly before a walk, growling softly while stretching, whimpering when he’s left alone. I started wondering—what if I could teach a machine to understand that? What if we could hear how dogs feel?

That curiosity turned into this project: a sound-based emotion classifier for dog vocalizations. I wanted to build something that felt personal, technically meaningful, and maybe even practically useful. I’m especially grateful to my professor, Zachary Lubberts , for his guidance and encouragement throughout this project. It’s an amazing feeling to work on something that genuinely excites you—where you’re constantly uncovering new challenges and trying to solve them.

This project was just the beginning, and it left me with even more questions: Could I reverse the process and generate emotionally representative barks? Could audio features be combined with video or behavior maps to improve accuracy? Could this kind of system actually help dog owners better understand or care for their pets?

There’s still a lot to explore—technically and emotionally—and I’m excited to keep building on this foundation.
